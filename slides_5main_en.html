Method
Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization (QUBO) for cell-type-specific gene selection
▶ What is QUBO? A binary optimization framework — minimize a single objective function H(x) where each gene is either selected (1) or not (0). Our H(x) has three terms:
H(x) = −s⊤x + γ · x⊤R x + λ (Σx − K)2
① Pick informative genes
si = edgeR |t|²
② Penalize correlated genes
Rij penalty → redundancy control
③ Fixed panel size K
K ∈ {10, 20, 30} auto by inner CV

Results 2 — Selected genes per cell type (CSF)
QUBO-selected genes in CSF — selection stability across cell types and validation panels
▶ Interpretation: each cell-type panel reflects biologically coherent programs. B = plasma / IgM (IGHM); Mono = iron metabolism (FTL) + MHC II (HLA-DPB1); NK = cytotoxic (KLRB1/KLRC1/CCL5); dnT = cytotoxic + Type I IFN (GZMA, ISG15); gdT = cytotoxic (GZMA, CD69). CD4_T/CD8_T/DC yielded no stable selection, likely reflecting signal dilution in heterogeneous pseudobulk populations (greyed columns).
  • Red boxes = genes mapped to an MS-relevant biology category (right-side green labels). Selected by biology, not by raw frequency (e.g., Mono CST3 = 100% is a generic cell-identity marker and was excluded).
▶ External validation against published signatures: Compared with the pathogenic B-cell signature (Ramesh et al. 2020, PNAS) — all 13 genes retained in our candidate pool were independently recovered by QUBO. Similarly, 9/12 genes from a CSF immune-dynamics signature were captured — supporting the biological validity of the selected panels.
6
Conclusion
QUBO delivers biology and biomarker simultaneously
1
Biomarker performance
QUBO_hybrid: CSF AUC 0.858, F1 0.731, MCC 0.427. #1 on F1 / MCC among 7 methods (imbalance-robust), #2 on AUC behind DE_top.
2
Biological validity
Mono captures MHC II + iron metabolism, NK/dnT/gdT capture the cytotoxic axis, B cells capture immunoglobulin-related programs — captured known MS-associated immune programs.
3
Cross-cohort reproducibility
σ_AUC 0.108 across 3 cohorts (top-2 among supervised methods). Heming (balanced) 0.738; Pappalardo / Ramesh 0.89-0.95 — supporting translational potential via clinically relevant reproducibility.
4
Methodological novelty
To our knowledge, among the first frameworks to jointly optimize relevance × non-redundancy × cardinality for cell-type-specific scRNA-seq biomarker design — explicitly modeling redundancy, unlike DE-top and standard sparse selection approaches.
Implication
QUBO provides a unified framework for jointly evaluating biomarker reproducibility and biological coherence in MS scRNA-seq data — informing both translational potential and mechanistic discovery.
▶ Future Work (Phase 2) — Resolving T-cell signal attenuation
  • Limitation: Pseudobulk aggregation attenuates key T-cell signals (CD4 Th17, CD8 cytotoxic)
  • Approach: Multi-Instance Learning (MIL) — Each donor = bag of cells → Attention identifies disease-associated cell subsets (e.g., Th17-like, Trm/exhausted CD8)
  • Extension of QUBO: Gene selection → Cell coreset selection → Select informative cells per donor
