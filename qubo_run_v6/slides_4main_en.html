Background — technical challenge
Existing MS scRNA-seq classifiers collapse when training and test cohorts differ in technical conditions (10x chemistry, cryopreservation, sampling). Existing methods (DE-top, LASSO, HVG) cannot jointly optimize relevance, redundancy, and cardinality.
→ This work resolves the issue via QUBO-based cell-type-specific gene selection + cross-cohort validation. Solved classically by Simulated Annealing; the same formulation runs on D-Wave quantum annealers (future-proof).

Method
QUBO formulation for cell-type-specific gene selection
H(x) = −s⊤x + γ · x⊤R x + λ (1⊤x − K)2
① Maximize relevance
Per-gene score si = |t-stat|² (from edgeR DEG). Prefer genes that differentiate MS from HD.
② Minimize redundancy
Pairwise correlation Rij penalizes co-selected similar genes. Avoids redundant pathway-mate selection — the key novelty of QUBO.
③ Cardinality constraint
Soft penalty toward K. K ∈ {10, 20, 30} auto-selected by inner 5-fold CV. Solver: Simulated Annealing (~3 sec/instance); deployable on D-Wave quantum annealers without modification.

3
Conclusion
Take-home Messages
1
Performance — wins on 3 primary metrics
CSF: AUC 0.788, F1 0.635, MCC 0.258. First place on AUC, F1, MCC among 5 methods; AP and σ_AUC marginally led by Elastic Net.
2
Cross-site reproducibility
Cohort-to-cohort std 0.044 (≈ EN 0.041; ~60% of LASSO 0.068). Tight 0.74-0.82 range across all three cohorts → stable on unseen sites.
3
Biological validity
MHC II antigen presentation (HLA-DRB1, HLA-DPB1, CD74, IFI30), Type I IFN (ISG15), cytotoxicity (GZMA, CCL5), iron metabolism (FTH1, FTL = lesion iron rim). GO MHC II p < 10⁻⁸.
4
Quantum-annealer compatible
Formulated as binary optimization → directly deployable on D-Wave quantum annealers. Classical SA already runs in ~3 sec/instance; transitions seamlessly as quantum hardware matures.
Implication for Clinical Translation
QUBO simultaneously satisfies five criteria: highest AUC × highest F1, MCC × cross-cohort stability on par with Elastic Net × biological validity × quantum-ready. As a cross-site-reproducible MS biomarker panel, it is a strong candidate for clinical translation.
▶ Future Work (Phase 2): Cell-level extension via Multi-Instance Learning (MIL): treat each donor as a bag of cells and apply attention to identify which cell subpopulations drive the prediction. QUBO retains its role for (i) gene selection, with the additional novel role of (ii) selecting informative cell coresets per donor. A joint gene-cell QUBO is planned for the D-Wave Leap Hybrid CQM Solver — also serving as direct quantum-annealer validation.
