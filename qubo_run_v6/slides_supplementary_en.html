Statistical considerations (EPV)
EPV (Events Per Variable): per cell-type classifier with K=17 × 22 events ≈ 1.3, below the classical Peduzzi 1996 threshold (≥10). However: (i) L2 regularization, (ii) 8-cell-type soft-voting, (iii) cross-cohort validation mitigate overfitting. Empirical cross-cohort σ_AUC = 0.044 (CSF) / 0.033 (PBMC) is direct evidence overfitting is not severe. Modern guidelines (van Smeden 2019, Riley 2019) accept EPV 2-5 for prediction modeling with regularization.

S7QUBO Formulation — Details
QUBO (Quadratic Unconstrained Binary Optimization)
▶ What is QUBO?: An optimization framework that expresses Yes/No (= 0/1) selection problems as a quadratic function. Here, "select gene i (1) or not (0)" is encoded into a single score that combines relevance (informative genes) × non-redundancy × cardinality (panel size), minimized to find the best gene combination. QUBO is a standard formulation in cheminformatics and combinatorial optimization, solvable with general-purpose solvers such as Simulated Annealing.
H(x) = −s⊤x + γ · x⊤R x + λ (Σixi − K)2
x ∈ {0,1}p: a binary variable per candidate gene (1 = selected, 0 = not), p ≈ 100 per cell type
① Relevance — −s⊤x
si = edgeR |t-statistic|2 for MS vs HD
→ genes with stronger MS-vs-HD signal score higher
Z-scored across all genes.
Selecting one lowers H = reward.
② Redundancy — γ · x⊤R x
Rij = |Spearman corr(gi, gj)| on training pseudobulk
→ co-expressed pairs incur a joint penalty
γ balances the magnitudes.
Enforces diversity — absent from LASSO/DE_top.
③ Cardinality — λ(Σx − K)2
K ∈ {10, 20, 30} auto-selected by inner 5-fold CV
→ quadratic penalty for deviating from K (soft)
λ ≫ relevance/redundancy magnitudes.
Panel size needs no post-hoc tuning.
▶ Why QUBO?
Any problem expressible with binary variables and a quadratic form is a universal substrate solvable on GPU SA, classical ILP, or (in the future) quantum hardware. With ~100 candidates per cell type, we use Simulated Annealing at ~3 sec/instance. Scaling the candidate pool to ~8,000 genes maps cleanly onto GPU-parallel SA.
▶ Why not fix K = 10?
Optimal K differs by cell type — B cells benefit from K=20-30 (IGH/IGL diversity), Mono is dominated by MHC II and saturates at K=10. Letting K be tuned by the same inner CV aligns panel size with cell-type biology. Empirical mean: ~17 genes per cell type.
▶ Implementation summary: qubo_pipeline_v6.py L89 K_GRID = [10, 20, 30] ／ Solver dwave-neal SimulatedAnnealingSampler (10 reads/seed) ／ γ, λ grid-searched in inner CV (γ ∈ {0.5, 1.0, 2.0}, λ = 10·max(s)) ／ Final K picked by validation AUC.

▶ Why the most abundant cell types yield no DEGs
① Functional subtype mixture: CD4 contains Th1/Th17/Treg/Tfh; CD8 contains Tem/Tcm/Trm/exhausted/MAIT. Only a few % subset (e.g. Th17, CD8 effector) drives MS pathology.

② Pseudobulk dilution: averaging all donor cells washes out the subset signal under 90% "ordinary" T cells.

③ edgeR's harsh BH-FDR: large cell counts tighten the dispersion estimate; subtle differences are detected but lost to multiple testing correction.

④ Biology filter: the residual signal is dominated by ribosomal/HK genes; removing them leaves the candidate pool nearly empty.
▶ Critical interpretation — this does NOT imply CD4/CD8 are MS-irrelevant
CD4 Th17 IL-17 axis, CD8 cytotoxic-mediated demyelination, and Treg dysfunction are central to MS pathobiology (Ramesh 2020, Schafflick 2020; IL7R/IL2RA are top MS GWAS hits). Our current panel captures the "wall" biology (B / Mono / NK axes) but misses the "core mechanism" (CD4 Th17, CD8 cytotoxic) due to pseudobulk dilution. Honest disclosure.
▶ Why MIL is essential for Phase 2: Multi-Instance Learning (MIL) operates at cell-level resolution. Attention identifies which cell subsets drive the disease — without pseudobulk averaging, surfacing Th17 within CD4 and Trm/exhausted within CD8 as attention weights. This directly resolves the largest limitation of the current study (T-cell dilution), which is the existence rationale for Phase 2 MIL.
