Why is cross-cohort MS classification challenging?
  • Multiple sclerosis (MS) affects ~2.8 million people worldwide; scRNA-seq is rapidly accelerating biomarker discovery
  • However, differences in technical conditions across sites (10x chemistry, cryopreservation, sampling protocol) cause classification accuracy to collapse
  • Existing methods (DE-top / LASSO / HVG) cannot jointly handle redundancy control and cardinality constraints, limiting cross-cohort reproducibility

This work: QUBO-based cell-type-specific gene panel design + 8-way soft-voting ensemble + early biology filter, achieving cross-site reproducibility

Method
QUBO formulation for gene selection
H(x) = −s⊤x + γ · x⊤R x + λ (1⊤x − K)2
① Maximize relevance
Sum gene-level "informativeness" si (= |t-stat|²) over selected genes — prefer genes that differ between MS and HD
② Minimize redundancy
Penalize selected pairs by their correlation Rij — avoid duplicating similar genes (ensures diversity)
③ Exactly K genes
Cardinality constraint via (Σxi − K)². Solver: Simulated Annealing (~3 s / instance)
Comparison of 5 methods at K = 20

Conclusion
Take-home Messages
1
Performance — wins on the 3 primary classification metrics
On CSF, AUC 0.788, F1 0.635, MCC 0.258 — all #1. Elastic Net edges it on AP and σ_AUC by very small margins (Δ 0.024 / 0.003).
2
Cross-site reproducibility — on par with Elastic Net
Cohort-to-cohort std of 0.044 (Elastic Net 0.041, HVG 0.048, LASSO 0.068). QUBO converges to a tight 0.74-0.82 range across Pappalardo / Heming / Ramesh.
3
Biological validity — aligned with MS pathobiology
Recurrently selected genes: HLA-DRB1, HLA-DPB1 (MS GWAS), ISG15 (Type I IFN), GZMA (cytotoxicity), FTH1/FTL (iron metabolism). GO: MHC II antigen presentation p < 10⁻⁸.
4
Quantum-annealer compatible
Formulated as binary optimization, directly deployable on D-Wave quantum annealers. Classical SA already runs in ~3 s / instance — a future-proof design that transitions seamlessly as quantum hardware matures.
Implication for Clinical Translation
QUBO simultaneously satisfies "highest AUC × highest F1, MCC × cross-cohort stability on par with Elastic Net × biological validity × quantum-ready", positioning it as a strong candidate for cross-site-reproducible MS biomarker panels in clinical translation.
▶ Future work (Phase 2): Cell-level extension via Multi-Instance Learning (MIL). Each donor is treated as a bag of cells; attention mechanisms aggregate cells while preserving per-cell interpretability — identifying which cell subpopulations drive the disease prediction. QUBO retains its role for (i) gene selection, with the additional novel role of (ii) selecting informative cell coresets per donor. A joint gene-cell QUBO (~5,000 binary variables) is planned for implementation on the D-Wave Leap Hybrid CQM Solver, providing direct quantum-annealer validation.
