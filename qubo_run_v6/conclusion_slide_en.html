Conclusion
Take-home Messages
1
Performance — Wins on 4 of 5 metrics
On CSF, QUBO achieved AUC 0.781 ± 0.041, F1 0.640, and MCC 0.297. It ranks first on AUC, F1, MCC, and σ_AUC (4 of 5 metrics). Loses only AP to LASSO (Δ 0.013).
2
Cross-site reproducibility — lowest σ
Cohort-to-cohort std of 0.041 — the lowest among all methods (LASSO 0.057, DE_top 0.065). All three independent cohorts (Pappalardo / Heming / Ramesh) yielded held AUC within 0.74-0.82, demonstrating "stable performance on unseen study sites."
3
Biological validity — Aligned with MS pathobiology
Recurrently selected genes include HLA-DRB1, HLA-DPB1 (top MS GWAS risk loci), ISG15 (Type I IFN), GZMA (cytotoxic T/NK), and FTH1/FTL (iron metabolism). GO pathway analysis: MHC class II antigen presentation enriched at p < 10⁻⁸.
4
Quantum-annealer compatible
Formulated as a binary optimization, the same objective can be deployed on D-Wave quantum annealers without modification. Classical SA already runs in ~3 s per instance, providing a future-proof design that can seamlessly transition to quantum hardware as it matures.
Implication for Clinical Translation
QUBO simultaneously satisfies "high mean AUC × lowest cohort variance × biological validity × quantum-ready", positioning it as a strong candidate for cross-site-reproducible MS biomarker panels in clinical translation.
