Background
Why is cross-cohort MS classification challenging?
  • Multiple sclerosis (MS) is a chronic autoimmune disease affecting ~2.8 million people worldwide
  • Single-cell RNA-seq has accelerated the search for diagnostic biomarkers, but classification accuracy collapses when training and test cohorts differ in technical conditions

Limitations of Existing Methods
  • DE-top (top-|t| ranking): tends to pick redundant genes from the same pathway → cohort-dependent
  • LASSO / Elastic Net: K is controlled only indirectly through the regularization parameter λ; it cannot enforce exactly K = 20
  • HVG (highly variable genes): unsupervised → also picks batch-driven genes
  • Cell-level Wilcoxon (Seurat::FindMarkers): inflates false positives due to pseudoreplication (Squair et al. 2021)
Our Approach
  • Per-cell-type QUBO optimization: independent panel design across 8 immune cell types
  • Simultaneous optimization of relevance + redundancy + cardinality: three axes that existing methods cannot jointly handle
  • Direct cross-cohort generalization assessment via 3-cohort × LOOCV: quantifies cross-site reproducibility
  • Early biology filter: housekeeping/mito/ribosomal genes excluded before selection, ensuring biological validity of the panel
▶ Proposal: A QUBO-based framework for cell-type-specific gene panel design
By simultaneously optimizing relevance × non-redundancy × cardinality, we construct cross-cohort reproducible MS biomarker panels suitable for clinical translation.
